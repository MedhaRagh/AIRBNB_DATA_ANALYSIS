Introduction

We are a team of five - Thanuska S, Ambati Renuka, Akshaya Vajpeyarr, Cheekireddy Dhamini and Medha R. We present here our exploratory data analysis, visualization and other interesting insights into the Airbnb data. We focus on San Diego's data as we wish to perform an in-depth analysis on one of the most densely populated cities in the world.

Since 2008, guests and hosts have used Airbnb to expand on travelling possibilities and present' more unique, personalized way of experiencing the world. This dataset describes the listing activity and metrics in San Diego, California for 2019.


Description about the dataset

The data is sourced from the Inside Airbnb website http://insideairbnb.com/get-the-data.html which hosts publicly available data from the Airbnb site. This data includes all needed information to find out more about hosts, geographical availability, necessary metrics to make predictions and draw conclusions. The dataset comprises of three main tables:

Reviews This dataset had information regarding the reviews with respect to listing id.
Listings: This data had all the information regarding the listings. It had Host name, location, neighboardhood, price, monthly price, review score and number of reviews.
Calendar: This dataset had past booking data

The project is available in GitHub: https://github.com/MedhaRagh/Analyzing-the-Airbnb-Dataset

Following are a few questions that we aim to answer through our analysis:

1. Who are the top earners
2. Is there any relationship between monthly earnings and prices?
3. Any particular location getting maximum number of bookings?
4. Price relation with respect to location
5. Relationship between Quantity and price
6. Price vs amenities
7. Price vs location
8. Which are the popular neighborhoods, their average prices and no of listings?
9. How the pricing is varying with location, property type, reviews?
